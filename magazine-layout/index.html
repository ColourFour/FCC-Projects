Chongli Spartan

Spartan Ultra

Set against the breathtaking alpine scenery of Genting Snow Park in Chongli, Hebei—a venue that once hosted the Winter Olympic Games and sits at over 1,500 meters above sea level with 80 % mountainous terrain and 52 % forest cover— the Chongli Spartan Ultra is an unrelenting 50 kilometer obstacle course featuring more than 60 of Spartan's signature challenges, from brutal carries and rope climbs to wall scaling and mud crawls. Designed for the world's toughest endurance athletes, participants battle relentless elevation gain, unpredictable mountain weather, and the thrill of racing under the open sky before dawn breaks. Finishers earn a coveted custom belt buckle, a unique Ultra wedge to complete their Trifecta collection, and an exclusive finisher's T-shirt—symbols of a triumph few can claim. Embrace the Spartan creed, test your limits, and discover what you are truly made of at the Chongli Spartan Ultra.

Trifecta Weekend

The Chongli race weekend isn't just about the Ultra—it's a full Trifecta experience. Over two adrenaline-fueled days, racers can take on the Sprint (5K), Super (10K), and Beast (21K) to complete the legendary Spartan Trifecta. With each race offering unique terrain and obstacles, athletes face a true test of speed, strength, and stamina. Whether you're chasing your first Trifecta or adding another wedge to your collection, Chongli's high-altitude setting and world-class course design make it one of the most rewarding weekends on the Spartan calendar. Complete all three and join the global community of elite Spartans who push beyond limits.

Trail Run

The weekend before the Ultra, Chongli plays host to the Spartan Trail Race—an ideal tune-up event for those testing their endurance or seeking a pure mountain running experience. Featuring distances such as 10K, 21K, and 50K, the trail race winds through lush alpine forests and across rolling mountain ridgelines, offering technical terrain, thin air, and breathtaking views at every turn. With no obstacles—just you, the trail, and the climb—it's a raw and honest challenge that sharpens both legs and lungs for the Ultra ahead. Whether racing for time or soaking in the natural beauty, it's a must-run event in China's endurance calendar.
