John Snow (1813 - 1858)

English physician, pioneer of anesthesia and epidemiology

  • 1813 - Born on March 15 in York, England, the eldest of nine children.
  • 1827 - Apprenticed to a surgeon-apothecary in Newcastle upon Tyne.
  • 1832 - Treated cholera patients during the epidemic in northeast England — gained early interest in the disease.
  • 1836 - Moved to London to study medicine at the Hunterian School and University of London.
  • 1838 - Admitted as a member of the Royal College of Surgeons of England.
  • 1844 - Earned MD from the University of London.
  • 1848 - Begins pioneering work in anesthesia, especially with ether and chloroform.
  • 1849 - Publishes “On the Mode of Communication of Cholera”, arguing that cholera is waterborne.
  • 1854 - Investigates the Broad Street cholera outbreak in Soho, London. • Uses a map and statistics to trace the outbreak to a contaminated public water pump. • Persuades authorities to remove the pump handle, effectively ending the outbreak — considered the birth of modern epidemiology.
  • 1855 - Publishes a revised and expanded edition of his cholera book with detailed findings.
  • 1858 - Dies on June 16 at age 45 from a stroke.
